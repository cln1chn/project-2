CHILI OIL!

what is it?

history

Chinese chili oil developed after chili peppers reached China in the late 1500s. Chilies spread rapidly, especially in inland regions like Szechuan, where cooks infused dried peppers in hot oil. The result was a shelf-stable, flavorful condiment central to many regional cuisines.

description

Chili oil is a spicy condiment made by infusing dried chili flakes into hot oil, often with garlic, spices, or aromatics. It adds heat, aroma, and richness to dishes, enhancing noodles, dumplings, vegetables, and meats with a bold, savory kick.

ingredients

  • 2 cups of cooking oil
  • 5 grams of toasted sesame
  • 10 grams of Szechuan chilis

steps

  1. Heat cooking oil on medium-high for 5 minutes
  2. Slowly add Szechuan chilis and toasted sesame and reduce heat to medium
  3. Simmer for 20 minutes and enjoy!
